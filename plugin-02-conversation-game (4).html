Keys to an iterative conversation
1
Set a collaborative tone and decompose first
Tell the AI this is a thinking conversation, not a production task. Asking it to functionally decompose the task means you see its plan before it acts — and you can adjust it.
2
Answer the AI's questions properly
The AI's questions are there to sharpen its understanding. Skipping them produces generic output. Engaging with them produces something real and tailored to your class.
3
Share your real materials before creation begins
When you shift from brainstorm to creation, share your actual unit materials. That is what makes the output accurate and usable rather than generic.
4
Treat the first output as a starting point
Review what the AI produces with your professional knowledge. Specific, targeted feedback is always more efficient than asking it to start again.
